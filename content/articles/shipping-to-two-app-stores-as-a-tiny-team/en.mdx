Every release goes to two stores with different review processes, screenshot requirements, and rejection moods. Here's how we keep that manageable without a release manager.

## Automate the mechanical, checklist the judgment

Build numbers, changelogs, store uploads, and screenshots are scripted — a release starts with one command. What stays manual is judgment: a short checklist covering the things automation can't catch, like "does the new flow make sense in Portuguese?"

## Staged rollouts are non-negotiable

Android releases go out to 10% of users first. Twice, that first 10% caught a crash our tests missed — an old OS version, a locale-specific date format. Ninety percent of our users never saw either bug.

## Boring is the goal

A good release week is one where nothing interesting happens. We optimize for exactly that.
