Every app we ship at Mango Labs starts with the same question: **what is the one thing this app should do really well?**

It sounds obvious, but it's the opposite of how most software is built. Products tend to grow sideways — more features, more settings, more screens — until the original idea is buried under everything that was added later. We decided early on to go the other way.

## Small apps respect your time

A currency converter should convert currencies. A bill splitter should split bills. When you open Xchanger or Splitte, there is no onboarding carousel, no account wall, no feature tour — you land directly on the thing you came to do.

That focus has practical consequences:

- **Faster to learn.** If an app does one thing, the interface can explain itself.
- **Faster to use.** Fewer taps between opening the app and getting your answer.
- **Easier to trust.** A small surface area means fewer places for bugs — and fewer reasons to ask for your data.

## Constraints are a feature

Being a small independent studio could be seen as a limitation. We treat it as a design constraint that keeps us honest. We can't maintain a bloated product, so we don't build one. Every feature we add is a feature we'll be maintaining five years from now — that math makes you selective.

When we're unsure whether something belongs in an app, the default answer is _no_. Most ideas that survive that filter make the product sharper, not bigger.

## What this means for what's next

We'd rather ship another small, sharp tool than bolt a marketplace onto an existing one. The apps in our catalog — Proportion, Xchanger, SunRouter, Splitte — will keep getting refinements, and new apps will follow the same rule: one job, done really well.
