Splitting a bill is a thirty-second problem. Most apps turn it into a five-minute one: create an account, invite your friends, name the group, assign items. By the time you're done, someone already paid in cash.

## The dinner-table test

Every Splitte flow has to survive what we call the dinner-table test: can you finish it while people are putting on their coats? That killed accounts, sign-ups, and friend lists on day one. You open the app, type amounts, and show the screen.

## Rounding is a feature

Real bills don't divide evenly. Splitte rounds each share sensibly and puts the leftover cents on the person who split the bill — because they're the one holding the phone, and nobody argues over two cents with the person doing the work.

Software that respects a small problem stays small. That's the whole design philosophy.
