People are often surprised that a studio our size ships to iOS, Android, and the web simultaneously. The honest answer: we're ruthless about sharing code.

## One codebase, native feel

Our apps are built with React Native and TypeScript. The business logic — conversions, calculations, storage — is 100% shared. What we don't share is feel: navigation patterns, haptics, and platform conventions follow each OS. Users should never sense the framework.

## Boring dependencies, on purpose

Every dependency is a future maintenance bill. We pick boring, widely-used libraries and keep the list short. When a niche package would save us a day now but cost us a migration later, we write the fifty lines ourselves.

The stack isn't impressive on a conference slide. It's impressive in the update history: small team, steady releases, both stores, every time.
