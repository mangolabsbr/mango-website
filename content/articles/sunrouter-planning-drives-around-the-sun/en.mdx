SunRouter answers one question: on this drive, at this time, which side of the car does the sun hit? It sounds trivial until you try to compute it.

## Geometry meets geography

The sun's position depends on date, time, and where on Earth you are — and your heading changes at every turn of the route. SunRouter samples the route into segments, computes the solar azimuth for each one, and compares it against your direction of travel. The result is a simple summary: mostly left, mostly right, or switching sides at a specific point.

## Precision that matters, approximation where it doesn't

We use a proper solar-position algorithm because being off by a few degrees flips the answer near sunrise and sunset — exactly when people care most. But we don't pretend centimeter GPS accuracy matters for picking a seat. Knowing which precision is worth paying for is most of the engineering.
