We do collect analytics — pretending otherwise would be dishonest. The interesting part is what we decided not to collect.

## Counts, not profiles

We track that features are used, not who uses them. "Conversion screen opened 12,000 times this week" shapes our roadmap. Attaching that to a person wouldn't make the decision better — it would just make the data more dangerous to hold.

## The test we apply

Before adding any event, we ask: would a user shrug if they saw this in a log? "App opened" passes. "Converted 250 USD to BRL at 14:32" does not — so we don't record amounts, ever.

## Less data, clearer signal

A small, deliberate set of metrics is also easier to read. When every event answers a real question, dashboards stop being noise. Privacy and focus turn out to be the same discipline.
